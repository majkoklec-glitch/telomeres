Vetiver.

Vetiver is one of those materials that people either love or hate. It is a grass whose roots are used in perfumery. After harvesting they are dried, and the essential oil is extracted by steam distillation. Here I used Haitian vetiver — it smells dry, faintly citrusy. Some find it reminiscent of hazelnuts, others of dry autumn leaves. It is an extraordinarily complex material, and one I have a deep personal fondness for.

In this composition I paired vetiver with bergamot oil, a blueberry bush accord, and a touch of pink pepper oil. The base is rounded out with sweet musks and Indian sandalwood oil, which soften and smooth the whole composition.

Vetiver Parfum 50 ml

Composition

Top bergamot, pink pepper
Heart Haitian vetiver, blueberry bush accord
Base sweet musks, Indian sandalwood
